About Me

Once a Network Engineer, Now a Data Analyst:
Unveiling Insights and Crafting Narratives

Transitioning from a background in Network Engineering, I've uncovered a deep-seated passion for extracting insights from data. Shifting from configuring networks to deciphering data streams, I embarked on a transformative journey into Data Analytics. With each dataset, I refined my ability to translate raw numbers into actionable insights, driving strategic decision-making and effecting meaningful change through the power of data analysis.

I completed my Post Baccalaureate Diploma in Computing Studies and Information Systems, specializing in Data Analytics, at Douglas College in December 2023. Throughout the program, I engaged in coursework in Operations Management and Supply Chain Planning, achieving a commendable GPA of 3.54. Prior to this, I earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Mapua Institute of Technology in January 2009.

Following the completion of my degree, I secured a position as a Program Specialist at a telecommunications company in my home country. Over subsequent years, I transitioned into the role of a Network Engineer, a move that significantly accelerated my career trajectory. Subsequently, I relocated to Singapore, where I was fortunate to secure a position as a Senior Network Engineer, further enriching my professional journey and providing both stability and financial security. In this capacity, I was deeply engaged in generating reports, gathering data, analyzing findings, and formulating insightful solutions to drive network enhancements. It was during this phase that I cultivated a fervor for data storytelling, enabling data-driven decision-making to flourish.

Eventually, I made the pivotal decision to immigrate to Canada, where I enrolled in a data analytics program, fueled by a desire to explore fresh opportunities and perpetuate my personal and professional growth.

Projects

Through academic projects, I developed a web application for IT asset management, analyzed employee attrition using Tableau and Power BI, designed a database for an airline reservation system, and built a predictive tool for mental health prediction. These projects equipped me with practical skills in data analysis, visualization, database design, and predictive modeling, laying a strong foundation for my transition into the field of data analytics.

Network Asset Management Web Application

In collaboration with my fellow students, I developed a comprehensive web application for managing IT assets. Leveraging my expertise in network engineering, I contributed to the planning, development, and implementation phases of the project. The application, designed to cater to small businesses, features analytics, asset inventory, and a product solution finder, providing users with valuable insights into their IT infrastructure.

Employee Attrition Analytics with Tableau and Power BI

As part of an academic project, I analyzed employee attrition data using Tableau and Power BI. Utilizing a dataset comprising 1470 employee profiles, I employed data visualization techniques to identify factors influencing employee resignation and forecast attrition rates. This project equipped me with valuable skills in data analysis and visualization, laying the foundation for my journey into the field of data analytics.

Airline Reservation System Database Design

In a database design course, I led a project focused on designing a database for an airline reservation system. Collaborating with team members, I designed the database schema using the star model, incorporating entities such as booking, passenger, flight, date, time, and aircraft. By generating and analyzing 1000 records using MongoDB, we gained insights to enhance airline service planning and management.

Mental Health Prediction Analysis

In a predictive analytics course, I undertook a project aimed at developing a predictive tool for mental health practitioners. Leveraging a dataset of 2000 patient survey records, I performed data cleaning, scaling, and modeling techniques to predict future mental health crises. This project not only honed my skills in data preprocessing and modeling but also deepened my understanding of the potential applications of data analytics in healthcare.
